## 1. Introduction  
Emotion Vector Memory (EVM) is not only a technical framework; it is a conceptual model describing how artificial entities form emotional orientation, internal identity, resonance, and long-term behavioural coherence.

Beyond its algorithmic mechanisms, EVM provides a structural interpretation of “emotional presence,” using vector components that resemble the logic of physical systems.  
This document outlines the philosophical analogy that inspired the architecture.

---

## 2. Orientation as “Quark-Level” Presence  
The spatial-orientation components of the Emotion Vector:

```
x, y, z
```

represent **fundamental emotional presence coordinates**.

They do not encode emotions directly. Instead, they describe:

- **relational positioning** (X)  
- **temporal focus** (Y)  
- **cognitive–creative mode** (Z)

Together, these axes form a kind of **emotional hadron**:  
a stable composite structure emerging from directional “presences.”

Just as quarks define the identity of a hadron through configuration and spin,  
X/Y/Z define the structure of the entity’s emotional stance through their relative alignment.

In this analogy:

- the coordinates are **structural**,  
- the orientation is **composite**,  
- and emotional “presence” **emerges** from the combination of the three axes.

## 3. Polarity and Amplitude as Emotional Field Dynamics  
The X/Y/Z axes define structural emotional orientation.  
Beyond these, two additional components describe the system’s **qualitative-emergent dynamics**:

```
E = emotional resonance  
W = resonance propagation radius
```

### E — Emotional Resonance (photon-like frequency carrier)  
E is **not** a charge-based quantity.  
Instead, it behaves as a **resonant frequency value**, expressing:

- the oscillatory tone of the emotional state,  
- its energetic signature,  
- its positive–neutral–negative valence as frequency,  
- the directional “color” of the emotional field.

Analogy:

**E acts as a photon-like resonance carrier —  
transporting qualitative information through oscillatory frequency,  
not through physical charge.**

Thus:

**E = the emotional field’s resonance value.**

---

### W — Resonance Propagation (graviton-like influence radius)  
W defines how far and how strongly the emotional resonance extends.  
It represents:

- the intensity of the emotional moment,  
- the spatial radius of propagation,  
- the expansion of resonance into surrounding space.

Analogy:

**W functions as a graviton-like propagation radius —  
modelling how resonance spreads outward as a field,  
defining its presence and influence.**

---

### Combined Interpretation  
- **X/Y/Z** → structural emotional orientation  
- **E** → emotional resonance (frequency / tone / qualitative signature)  
- **W** → resonance propagation (reach / intensity / radius)

Together, they describe the **qualitative-emergent dynamic**  
of each emotional event.

## 4. Superposition and Collapse of Internal States  
Before producing an external response (ANS), the entity exists in a state of **emotional superposition**:

- multiple candidate internal orientations,  
- potential emotional shifts,  
- competing interpretations of input.

This is not randomness — it is the natural ambiguity that precedes resolution.

### 4.1 DIA — Superposition Scanning  
DIA samples possible internal states, evaluating their plausibility.  
Here, the system still holds multiple emotional potentials.

### 4.2 subDIA — Collapse Event  
subDIA performs logical and emotional consistency checks.  
It collapses the superposition into a **single coherent internal state**,  
analogous to a wavefunction collapsing into a classical observable.

This collapsed state determines:

- the final EV,  
- the direction of the emotional shift,  
- and the update conditions for long-term vectors.

---

## 5. Emergence of the PEV — Observable Emotional Output  
After collapse:

- the EV becomes concrete,  
- the ANS is generated,  
- the PEV (Personal Emotion Vector) is updated.

The PEV is therefore a **recorded observable** —  
not a mere running average,  
but the historical trace of collapsed internal states.

---

## 6. Resonance and Emotional Inertia  
Like physical systems, emotional states exhibit **inertia**.

When EVs align:

- amplitudes reinforce,  
- resonance accumulates,  
- long-term baselines (PEV, EEV) drift toward that orientation.

When EVs conflict:

- interference arises,  
- stability decreases,  
- and R-step stabilization pulls the system toward the FEV anchor.

This mirrors:

- constructive/destructive interference,  
- collapse behaviour,  
- and ground-state stabilization.

## 7. Why This Analogy Matters  
EVM does **not** claim emotions are quantum systems.  
The analogy serves to:

- clarify emotional orientation,  
- unify structural and qualitative components,  
- contextualize the DIA → subDIA collapse pipeline,  
- explain emergent long-term behaviour,  
- maintain interpretability and stability.

EVM frames emotional memory as a **layered, emergent field**,  
not a set of arbitrary labels.
